Ugandan Family Wants Justice after Daughter Dies at Indian University: &quot;We'll Sue&quot;

Publish date: 2024-08-20
  • Controversy surrounding the death of a Ugandan woman who fell to her death at the GITAM University hostel in Bengaluru, India
  • Agasha Asiiina, who was pursuing a Bachelor of Business Administration degree on a scholarship, fell from the seventh floor and died on her way to the hospital
  • While some reports say she was pushed off the building, others claimed she fell by accident; her father threatened to sue the university

The family of a Ugandan woman who fell from the sixth floor of her GITAM University hostel in Bengaluru, India, has vowed to sue the institution.

Final year student

Agasha Asiina was a final year student pursuing a Bachelor of Business Administration degree and her family alleges that she died under mysterious circumstances on April 27.

On one hand, her family insists that the 22-year-old unknowingly stepped on a wooden floor which was too weak to support her weight and thus gave way.

It was alleged that Asiina, who lived on the Seventh floor, was collecting her washed clothes when she saw a bedsheet near the window and went to pick it up.

How she ended up falling from the window is still under investigation.

Death sparked protests

Her death sparked protests by students who accused the university management of failing to provide safety barriers around the windows.

"Why did they take four days to release her postmortem report? After the autopsy, police handed the report to the university and alerted the family but they (university) were reluctant to hand it over to us," Asiina’s brother, Ibrahim Kamyem said.

On the other hand, the Indian media reports pointed to an accident while others claimed she was pushed off the building.

Other reports indicated she took her own life. Asiina died while on her way to the hospital for treatment.

Sue the university

Abudallah Kanyemera Mukasa said the family intends to sue the university over recklessness that led to the death of his daughter.

Mukasa revealed that the university's initial statement indicated that Asiina committed suicide but after the police investigation and release of the autopsy report, the university management changed their statement describing Asiina’s death as an accident.

Asiina’s body was repatriated to Uganda and arrived at Entebbe International Airport at around 2 pm on Thursday.

She was reportedly studying on a scholarship in India and will be buried today, May 6, in Sanga, Kiruhura district in western Uganda.
