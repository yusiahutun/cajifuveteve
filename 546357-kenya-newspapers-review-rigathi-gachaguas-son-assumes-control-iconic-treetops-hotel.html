Kenya Newspapers Review: Rigathi Gachagua's Son Assumes Control of Iconic Treetops Hotel

Publish date: 2024-03-05

On Saturday, April 27, the local newspapers widely reported on the ongoing rainfall across the country that has continued to leave death and destruction in its wake.

1. Saturday Nation

The publication revealed that the iconic Treetops and Outspan hotels are set to reopen in May, charting a new era for the hospitality sector under the proprietorship of Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua’s son.

This time, the Treetops Hotel, famed for witnessing Queen Elizabeth's ascension to the throne 72 years ago, will also be a significant relic of the Mau Mau movement in Nyeri.

According to Keith Rigathi, the new hotel owner and son of DP Gachagua, the establishment holds a significant part of Kenya’s history on its liberation struggle that has remained untold.

“For instance, very few people know that beneath the hotel lie underground tunnels constructed by the Mau Mau fighters for accessing the facility,” he explains.

Located roughly 10 kilometers from the hotel, within the Aberdare Forest, is a fig tree that served as a post office for the Mau Mau movement.

The DP hinted at the absorption of the hotels on April 4 at the Nyeri National Polytechnic's fourth graduation ceremony.

He said that the decision to buy the two historic hotels stemmed from a challenge that he had shared with the local business people.

2. The Star

The paper reported that the ongoing rains claimed the first victim in Naivasha after a quarry caved in, killing a middle-aged man in the Maraigushu area of Naivasha.

During the incident, scores of other workers escaped death narrowly as the rains continued to pound the area, raising fears that more quarries could collapse.

A witness, James Mwai, said that a section of the quarry caved in, and in the process, a boulder clashed with the victim, killing him on the spot.

"The area has recorded heavy rains in the last week and this could have caused the accident and we have asked those working here to relocate," he said.

Naivasha OCPD Stephen Kirui confirmed the incident, adding that the body was taken to the local mortuary, and an inquest file was opened.

Meanwhile, one person died, and over 60 others escaped death narrowly after a bus destined for Kampala, Uganda, lost control along the Nairobi-Nakuru before landing into a ditch.

During the early morning incident in Kayole, Naivasha, scores of the passengers escaped with minor injuries while hailing the driver for saving their lives.

The accident occurred at the same spot where another bus driver lost control and killed over ten students and staff last year.

3. Taifa Leo

The Swahili Daily reported an investigation revealed that the internet communication waves of the free Wi-Fi launched at various stations across the country by the Kenyan government are not functioning.

In some areas such as Kisumu, Siaya, Kwale, Mombasa, Laikipia, and Turkana Counties, the Wi-Fi systems either stopped working a few months after being launched or, if operational, the internet connection is not stable. In Kwale county, traders at Diani market complain of not benefiting from the programme.

"We had hoped that the program would boost our businesses, but we haven't seen its benefits. The free Wi-Fi stopped working in October last year," said Sebastian Juma, a trader at the market. In Mombasa County, the program stopped functioning six months ago.

On the other hand, traders in Nairobi, Turkana, and Trans Nzoia counties have also voiced their complaints about the government initiative, stating that they have not benefited from it.

4. The Saturday Standard

The paper reported tension remains high along the West Pokot-Elgeyo Marakwet border after suspected bandits raided Kamologon village and shot dead a seven-year-old girl.

Authorities in West Pokot County said the armed bandits stole an unknown number of livestock during the attack.

Police said the slain girl, identified as Shirleen Juliasia, was in the company of three other children who were herding livestock near the border to pursue the suspects, adding that the Anti-Stock Theft Unit Police have taken up the matter.

West Pokot County Commissioner Khaliff Abdullahi said the bandits invaded the village and ambushed local herders.

"The suspects made away with an unknown number of cattle," the commissioner said.

He said security officers in West Pokot had liaised with their Elgeyo Marakwet counterparts.

Area leaders who condemned the killings and cattle theft called on the government to upgrade security roads in the area.
