Even though modern culture has intertwined the phrases to mean the same thing, their true meaning and the reason the names are different would give you a good idea. So, what does each word mean in contemporary society?

What are dreadlocks?

Dreadlocks are an excellent style that involves backcombing, braiding, or rolling without much effort to finish the strands. Therefore, when an individual prefers to wear dreadlocks, they must be ready to undergo a styling process.

Everything starts with combing the strands of hair into a rope-like structure. And you can create different types of dreadlocks depending on how you do this procedure.

Evidence shows that people have been rocking this style since ancient days. So, where did dreadlocks originate?

Some sources claim that dreadlocks originated from Egypt, while others state that the style has some Greek origins. There is even another hypothesis that states Indians were the first culture to wear dreadlocks. Unfortunately, this theory does not have empirical evidence.

The most substantial theory with credible evidence is of Egyptian origin. A French Egyptologist discovered the remains of a mummy tightly glued on a dreadlock wig dating back to 1400 BCE. This evidence indicates that the dreadlocks style has its roots in Egypt.

On top of this, the Old Testament recounts the tale of Samson and Delilah, whereby a man's potency is linked to "the seven locks on his head." Also, according to Roman accounts, the celts possessed "hair like snakes." Greeks and Vikings are also said to have worn dreadlocks.

On the other hand, Rastafarians started wearing dreadlocks for a different reason. Everything started in the 1930s when Ras Tafari was crowned the emperor of Ethiopia. However, he was forced into exile during an invasion, and the guerrilla warriors swore not to cut their hair until he was reinstated. And that is how the Rastafari religion started and resonated with the day's ideologies.

What are locs?

Since the terms are used interchangeably, it is hard to distinguish between them. Locs are visually like dreads. The hairstyle is of African-Nubian descent and looks like a rope. So, why are they called locs, not dreads?

Locs undergo the same locking process as dreads, albeit slightly different. One of the essential things for locs is the pleasing after-effect or polished appearance. The buttoning stage is a second process that gives the hairstyle its unique appearance.

The tighter the hair, the better and faster the hair will lock. One of the most significant differences between dreads and locs is that with locs, you can see the roots of the hair strands, while you cannot see anything with dreads.

Dreads vs locs – what is the difference?

The term “locs and dreadlocks difference" has been widely searched as people try to get facts before going to the salon. You are likely still trying to understand the two hairstyles from the abovementioned definitions. So, what is the primary distinction between the two popular hairstyles?

The most notable difference between locs and dreads is that one is a hairstyle while the other is a lifestyle. It would be best to remember that locs are cultivated, but dreadlocks are not. Dreadlocks often stem from the Rastafarian religion and beliefs.

There is also a physical difference between the two hairstyles. If you have noticed, the loc hairstyle comes out cleaner and prettier than dreadlocks that appear more relaxed and somewhat fuzzy. Dreads have a more natural appearance, while locs are polished and shiny. Locs have more defined roots, while dreads don’t.

On top of the physical and cultural differences, people use the terms interchangeably. According to history, colonialists found the natural hairstyle dreadful, which led to the term dreadlocks. People these days prefer to refer to the phrase as locs. Therefore, they might mean dreads or locs.

Dreadlocks history in slavery

Aside from the general confusion between dreads and locs, there is a negative connotation. People tend to utilize locs, staying away from the word dreadlocks because it has a negative sense.

Many believe the term dreadlocks originated from the slave trade era when Africans lacked resources to groom their hair, forming the strands referred to as dreads. Their hair would get locked since the long voyages didn't allow them to carry out customary grooming rituals.

Because of their rope-like nature, the enslavers would refer to the hair as dreadful. It was later referred to as dreadlocks. Therefore, people would like to avoid the term because of its painful history.

What do Jamaicans call dreadlocks?

While the rest of the world uses the term dreads or locs, Jamaicans add another phrase to make it suit their culture. They use the term nutty dreadlock. The country has a rich Rastafarian culture, and people who conform to the religion wear dreadlocks, hence the "nutty" phrase.

Are dreadlocks cultural appropriation?

Dreadlocks are seen as a form of cultural appropriation when worn by Caucasian people because of their deep connection to African culture. Scholarly research shows that dreadlocks were worn as a symbol of black resistance. Therefore, when worn by white people as a trendy hairstyle, it is a perception that colonizers are being blasé about adopting a hairstyle that is an expression of resistance.
