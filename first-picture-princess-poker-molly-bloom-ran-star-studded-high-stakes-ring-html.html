Poker princess: Molly Bloom hosted exclusive high-stakes games allegedly frequented by a string of Hollywood's finest

She was once the princess of poker who hosted exclusive high-stakes games allegedly frequented by a string of Hollywood's finest.

But when the chips went down and the poker ring was exposed, after convicted ponzi schemer Bradley Ruderman lost more than $4million of company money in the games, Molly Bloom found herself in court testifying against him.

A-listers who allegedly played in the games were also exposed.

They included Tobey Maguire, Leonardo Dicaprio, Matt Damon, Ben Affleck, Macaulay Culkin, tennis ace Pete Sampras and Alexander Rodriguez.

A-Rod's rep Richard Rubenstein denies the star was involved but baseball officials are investigating the allegations.

It was in Los Angeles in the late 2000s when Bloom's poker games really took off, according to the New York Post.

She would invite a carefully selected group of around eight people to gamble through the night at the homes of players or in posh hotel suites.

The 33-year-old brought in sexy bottle-service girls from Hollywood clubs to serve drinks, while her dealers were aces from local casinos.

'There would be a quarter-million dollars on the table, easily, all in cash,' a source told the Post.

Soon the then 20-something from Colorado was racking up $400,000 a year for herself.

Childhood: Bloom grew up in Loveland, Colorado and her parents, let her and her two younger brothers spend most of their time on Keystone ski mountain

Quite something for a former ski-bum.

Bloom grew up in Loveland, Colorado and her parents, Larry, a clinical psychologist, and Charlene, a ski instructor, had let Bloom and her two younger brothers spend most of their time on Keystone ski mountain.

But by 18 she was in trouble with the law and was busted by police at her Colorado State University dorm for unreasonable noise and serving alcohol to minors.

Trouble: Bloom was busted by police at her Colorado State University dorm for unreasonable noise and serving alcohol to minors when she was 18

The year after she spent 30 days in prison after pleading guilty to speeding and in 1999 and 2001 was caught speeding again and having expired licence plates, according to court records.

She moved to Hollywood in 2003 and got a job assisting a real-estate mogul, who also happened to run high-stakes poker games.

She paid attention and made connections with the A-list players, according to the Post.

Involved: Ben Affleck is thought to have sat at Bloom's poker table around the time the actor was engaged to Jennifer Lopez in 2004

High profile: Tennis ace Pete Sampras, left, and Macauley Caulkin, right, are also thought to have played in the ring

'She was entrepreneurial and a very smart girl,' Ronald Richards, a celebrity attorney in Beverly Hills who represents Bloom, told the paper.

Bloom started running her own games, eventually luring her former boss' players to her own table.

Under California law, poker is legal if played in private homes, but individuals can't explicitly organise games unless they are licensed and paying taxes.

Lawyer, Richards told the Post Bloom made her money from 'tips.'

Also accused: Tobey Maguire, seen here with his wife, is said to have won millions in the high-stakes games

Other A-list Hollywood celebrities alleged to have played the no-limit Texas Hold ‘em games include Matt Damon, left, and Leonard DiCaprio

She is thought to have hosted three to four events a week and the guest list was always top secret, according to Richards.

The 'tip' money afforded Bloom several luxury apartments in West Hollywood. She also hired an assistant and, according to sources, dated Drew McCourt, whose father owns the Los Angeles Dodgers.

In 2009 she moved the enterprise to New York and it all changed.

She had fewer connections in the city and raids on underground games were common.

Also the stakes were far lower than Bloom's games in LA.

In June last year the pretty 5'3" brunette was hit with a $116,133 charge for declaring her 'catering' income from New York events but not paying taxes on it.

Sources revealed to the New York Post that two Eastern European 'thugs' broke into Bloom's apartment, beat her up but left when she handed over thousands of dollars in cash, last Autumn.

And then the poker princess had to deal with the fall-out of Ruderman.

She had hoped that agreeing to testify would get her off the hook, but was slapped with a suit by bankruptcy trustees in March.

She was accused of receiving $473,000 from Ruderman's City National Bank accounts, allegedly to settle his debts, according to the Post.

Bloom is now thought to have left New York for California, according to her doorman.

Her lawyer said she is lying low until her name is cleared. 'Hopefully it was just an embarrassing period and nothing more,' Richards added.
