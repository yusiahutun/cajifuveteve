Premier League winner left without a club after being released on Deadline Day

Publish date: 2024-01-28

Kasper Schmeichel has waved goodbye to Nice by mutual consent - a year after he was said to be 'infuriated' by his status at the club.

Schmeichel, who lifted the Premier League and FA Cup during his 11 seasons with Leicester, joined the Ligue 1 outfit on a free transfer last summer and made 46 appearances in all competitions.

The short statement read: "OGC Nice and Kasper Schmeichel have agreed to terminate the Danish international's contract by mutual consent.

"The 36-year-old goalkeeper arrived in Nice last summer and made 46 appearances for the club in all competitions. The club wishes him every success for the rest of his career."

His decision to accept free agency on Deadline Day comes almost a year after French media reported that he arrived with a 'very high' body fat percentage with former boss Lucien Favre leaving him on the bench.

There was further strain put on the relationship when Schmeichel's entourage reportedly went above Favre to ask club owner Sir Jim Ratcliffe about starting the first game of the season against Toulouse.

Schmeichel recently entered another pathway when appearing on Monday Night Football as a pundit alongside Jamie Carragher.

Schmeichel's connection with Brendan Rodgers could lead to a move to Scotland following their shared FA Cup triumph.

The keeper said last year: "For me, Brendan is one of the best managers I’ve ever seen up close so I only have good things to say about him."
