Tiles prices in Kenya: How much do they cost per square metre?

Publish date: 2024-06-10

Tiles are affordable, durable, versatile, easy to install, and fit all floor design styles. That's why they're a top-pick flooring installation option for many homeowners. There are various tiles to choose from, i.e., ceramic, porcelain, etc., and each has its unique aesthetic and price. Knowing the tiles prices in Kenya will help you choose the ideal one for your home.

Tiles are made from different materials and colors and are cut into many shapes. For these reasons, they have a wide price range, from high-end to affordable. Luckily, regardless of your budget and style preferences, there is a tile design and type that you can work with. Check the different tile options and their prices before starting your project.

How much is floor tiles per square metre in Kenya?

The average price of tiles in Kenya ranges between Ksh 900 to Ksh 4,500 per square metre. As mentioned, these prices vary depending on the type, size, color, and origin.

Types of tiles and their prices

Tiles are made of several materials. When choosing the right type of tile for your home, it's best to consider your budget, style, tile maintenance needs, durability, moisture resistance, and more. Here are the different types of stoneware to choose from and their prices.

Ceramic

These are among the most common, and that is why many people ask for ceramic tiles prices in Kenya. They cost between Ksh 900 to Ksh 2100 and are available in local stores or hardware in various patterns, styles, and colors.

Ceramics are clay-based, and their finish is usually textured and matte to offer excellent traction. They are strong, waterproof, and thus can withstand heavy floor traffic and humidity.

Porcelain

Porcelain is a finer type of clay ceramic. Unlike ceramic, it's made from high-quality clay and subjected to a different production process; this makes it much harder and better quality. Porcelains have a great cement-like and wood-look finish and are available in various textures.

Porcelain tiles are popularly used in floors, showers, and walls because they are durable, affordable, and don't chip or crack easily. Their price ranges from Ksh 1,500 to Ksh 2500 per square meter.

Granite

These are made from granite rock which results from the cooling of lava. You'll love their naturally appearing flecks and specks of color. Granite tiles are well-polished and aesthetically pleasing.

They are also less prone to cracks and chips and are waterproof, which makes them an excellent choice for wet areas such as kitchen countertops. These tiles cost around Ksh 900 to Ksh 1800 per square meter in Kenya.

Travertine

Travertine tiles are made from natural stone that's similar to limestone. Naturally, the travertine has tunnels and holes, but they are usually filled using epoxy resin and sealed before they are ready for installation. Travertine stands out because of its non-slip texture and unique and natural appearance, making them ideal for rustic style aesthetics.

Their major disadvantage is that they're soft and porous, thus only ideal for decorative spaces rather than floors. Additionally, they quickly get scratched; therefore, you must be more cautious when dealing with them. The travertines cost around Ksh 1000 to Ksh 1,500 per square meter.

Pebble and stone

Try the pebble and stone tiles if you're after an interior or exterior with a harmonized look. They come in different finishes and textures and are strong enough for places with high foot traffic. You'll love their natural patterns and appearance. Their price ranges between Ksh 2000 and Ksh 2,500 per square metre.
