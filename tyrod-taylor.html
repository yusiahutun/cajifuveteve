Tyrod Taylor is an American football player for the National Football League’s New York Giants (NFL).

He attended Virginia Tech and led the Hokies to the 2009 Orange Bowl as a sophomore and the 2011 Orange Bowl as a senior. The player was drafted in the sixth round of the 2011 NFL Draft by the Baltimore Ravens.

He served as the backup quarterback to starting quarterback Joe Flacco. Tyrod also did during the Ravens’ Super Bowl XLVII victory over the San Francisco 49ers.

In 2015, Taylor was signed as a free agent by the Buffalo Bills, taking over as starting quarterback that season and making the Pro Bowl.

In 2017, the player helped the Bills reach the playoffs for the first time in 17 years. In March 2018, he was traded to the Cleveland Browns, and the Bills drafted Josh Allen to replace him.

Tyrod went on to start for the Browns, Los Angeles Chargers, and Houston Texans. However, the player was sidelined by injuries and replaced by rookies Justin Herbert, Baker Mayfield, and Davis Mills.

Tyrod Taylor: College Career

Taylor made his college football debut on September 8, 2007, against the LSU Tigers. The player completed 7-of-18 passes for 62 yards and rushed 44 yards on nine attempts. 

Tyrod was supposed to be a redshirt freshman in 2008. However, following Virginia Tech’s loss to East Carolina University, his redshirt was removed due to Tech’s need for more offense.

Likewise, Taylor led the Hokies to a 9-3 regular-season record and a second-place finish in the ACC’s Coastal Division during his junior season.

In the 2009 Chick-fil-A Bowl, the Hokies defeated the Tennessee Volunteers 37-14, finishing with a 10-3 record.

Taylor’s senior season began with heartbreaking losses at home to Division I-FCS James Madison and at FedExField to Boise State. However, things got better by the end.

The player was named ACC Player of the Year and ACC Championship Game Most Valuable Player at the end of the regular season.

Similarly, Tyrod also took part in the East-West Shrine Game in 2011. He finished four of five passes for 59 yards.

Injuries and Setbacks

Like most other NFL players, Tyrod also had to go through his share of injuries during his playing career. 

Taylor left his first preseason start against the Atlanta Falcons with a shoulder injury. Early tests revealed no separation, and his injury was classified as a contusion.

Similarly, during his Week 6 with the Buffalo Games, Taylor suffered an MCL injury. 

Tyrod remained inactive for the final game of the 2016 season due to a contract provision that guaranteed his 2017 salary if he suffered a severe injury.

Likewise, Taylor had surgery on January 5, 2017, to repair a core muscle problem. The player was also chosen as a 2017 Pro Bowl alternate, but he declined due to injury.

In addition, Tyrod was limited to 65 passing yards against the New England Patriots in Week 13. He was escorted off the field after aggravating a knee injury in the first quarter.

The player was inactive for Week 14 due to a knee injury, but he returned as the starter in Week 15.

Further, In 2020, Taylor was hospitalized after suffering a rib injury during pre-game warmups the following week before a game against the Kansas City Chiefs. 

Days later, it was revealed that the Chargers’ team doctor accidentally punctured Taylor’s lung before the game while administering a painkiller. 

The player did not get a break from the injuries. The following year, Taylor suffered a hamstring injury in the first half of Week 2’s game against the Cleveland Browns and was replaced by rookie quarterback Davis Mills for the remainder of the game.

On September 21, he was placed on injured reserve. The player was, however, activated on November 6.

Are you pronouncing Taylor’s first name wrong?

A minor controversy on how to pronounce the NFL player’s first name arose after joining the Browns and being featured on Hard Knocks.

Many people, including Taylor’s father, TV analysts, and coaches, had been saying “TIE-rod” for years. 

However, Browns guard Joel Bitonio and fourth-string quarterback Brogan Roback were heard on the show saying “tuh-ROD.”

Taylor’s mother and the Browns’ senior vice president of operations agreed with the pronunciation. 

When asked about it, Taylor joked that while he had grown up hearing “TIE-rod,” his mother gave him the name “tuh-ROD,” but she “doesn’t call [him] by [his] first name.”
