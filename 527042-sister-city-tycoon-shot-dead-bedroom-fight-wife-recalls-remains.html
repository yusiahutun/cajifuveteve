Sister of City Tycoon Shot Dead in Bedroom after Fight with Wife Recalls Seeing His Remains

Publish date: 0001-01-01
  • Henry Katanga, a city businessman, was found dead in his bedroom with a gunshot injury to his head after a heated altercation with his wife
  • During Katanga's requiem mass, his sister, Naomi, revealed that she had spoken to him just moments before his death to arrange a meeting
  • She recalled the heartbreaking moment when she saw her brother's body lying in the morgue and even prayed for his resurrection

TUKO.co.ke’s Current Affairs journalist, Racheal Nyaguthie, has spent more than four years reporting on politics and crime stories in Kenya and covered the 2022 Presidential Election

Kampala- The sister of a city businessman who was recently found dead in his bedroom with a gunshot injury on his head after a heated altercation with his wife, has painfully recalled seeing his remains.

Henry Katanga, 61, died by gunshot on the morning of Thursday, November 2, at his home in upscale Mbuya Hill, outskirts of Kampala city, after what his daughter described as a fight with his wife.

Naomi, the bereaved sister, said she was now the only surviving sibling.

Henry Katanga's remains

She also recalled the heartbreaking moment she saw her brother's body lying in the morgue, saying she even prayed for his resurrection.

"At first, I refused to believe it. I went to Mbuya, and later, I went with the body to the mortuary. When they finally allowed me into that room, I went and grabbed Henry and prayed to God to resurrect him and take me instead," she said during the mass, as quoted by Pulse.Ug

A heartbroken Naomi prayed that God avenge her brother's death, emotionally insisting his blood will not "spill in vain".

"God will give us justice," she said.

Katanga's death

Moments after his death on Thursday, the Ugandan police, led by Deputy Inspector General of Police Katsigazi Tusiime, showed up at the scene to secure it ahead of their investigations work.

They found Katanga's lifeless body soaked in a pool of blood in his main bedroom with a gunshot wound on the head, reported Trumpet News.

He was positively identified by his daughter Tricia, whose curiosity was aroused by the loud gunshot; she found blood oozing from her father's head.
