Angelina Jolie and Billy Bob Thornton relationship explained

Publish date: 2024-07-13

Angelina Jolie and Billy Bob Thornton were among the most-talked-about Hollywood celebrity couples in the early 2000s. The two sweethearts always left the majority of their fans in admiration with their deep affection, even in public places. Most of them expected the pair to have a long-lasting union, but that did not happen. What happened to their relationship?

At times, love can make someone do the unthinkable. After two years of engagement, Bob Thornton quit his relationship to be with his other new found love, Angelina Jolie. Similarly, in less than three years, Jolie had already tattooed the name of the actor/singer on her arm.

Their relationship was not only extraordinary to many but also strange to others. Many headlines were created to describe their love life. In one instance, Rolling Stone magazine once defined their union as dangerous. The title on the cover read, Inside America's Most Dangerous Marriage.

1999: First encounter

The two met in a cast of Pushing Tin in 1999. Angelina was staring as Marry Bell while Billy Bob was portraying Russell Bell. In other words, they featured as a couple in the comedy-drama film. Little did they knew they were destined to be a pair a few months later.

Both of them had their histories. Billy Bob had married five times before he met with Angelina. Furthermore, during that time, he was engaged to Laura Dern – an actress and filmmaker. On the other hand, Angelina had divorced her first husband and was seeing Timothy Hutton, who is an actor and director.

July 2001: Angelina Jolie and Billy Bob Thornton blood vial

Their marriage became interesting with time. Due to their busy work schedules, they devised means of feeling close to each other even when they were far apart. Angie came with the idea of necklaces.

They sliced their fingers and smeared their blood in the lockets. They exchanged them, and they had to wear the necklaces around their necks all the time. To them, it was a romantic gesture.

Again, Angelina Jolie and Billy Bob Thornton necklaces put their relationship on the spotlight. Some considered the idea weird because of the blood vials. Addressing the issue in an interview with People magazine, the actor said,

2002: Plan to adopt a child

Two years into their marriage, the stars announced that they were planning to adopt a child. They reportedly travelled to Cambodia together. While there, they visited an orphanage. They found a young and handsome boy, Maddox, whom Angie adopted.

Later, it was revealed that Angie adopted Maddox as a single mother as opposed to their initial plan – joint adoption. It now became clear that the two were having some issues with their marriage.

2002 – 2003: Trouble in paradise

After the failed joint adoption in 2002, the stars started to have problems with their marriage. Some speculated that Billy had cheated on her with a sex therapist. However, no one commented on the infidelity issue.

Unable to tolerate each other anymore, the two called it quits. They initiated plans for divorce, which were finalized in March 2003. The two reportedly went their separate ways due to their incompatible lifestyles.

2005: Erasing the past memories

Does Angelina Jolie still have Billy Bob tattoo? No. In 2005, the beauty decided to remove the “Billy Bob” tattoo from her arm. It was removed using laser technology. To some extent, she thought it was a bad idea to have the tattoo of her ex-husband. The beauty swore never to tattoo the name of a man on her body again.

Moving on

What happened to Billy Bob Thornton and Angelina Jolie? After separating in 2003, the two have since moved on with their lives. They have remarried once. The actor has been happily married to Connie Angland since 2014, and they have a daughter. Currently, Billy Bob Thornton spouse is a puppeteer.

Jolie, on the other hand, married Brad Pitt. They jointly adopted two more kids (Zahara and Pax). She also gave birth to a daughter in 2006. Further, she gave birth to twins (Knox and Vivienne) in 2008.
