Hilary Farr, who was born in Toronto, spent her formative years in London, where she first followed her love of dance at the Royal dance School until she was 11 years old. She started helping her mother decorate their home, though, and her interests turned to theater and interior design.

In Los Angeles, where she worked as a home renovator and also dabbled in setting design for film and television productions, Farr's career really took off. She dabbled in acting and made brief appearances in important movies including The Rocky Horror Picture Show (1975), City on Fire (1979), and The Return (1980) in addition to her work behind the scenes.

Farr developed her design and remodeling abilities throughout the years, becoming a well-known architect in the process. She gained notoriety in 2010 as the co-host of the popular program Love It or List It, whose original premise was to assist homeowners in deciding whether to renovate their present home or buy a new one. The success of the show was greatly aided by her dynamic cooperation with David Visentin and her knowledge in design and renovation.

Along with running Hilary Farr's Designs, a well-known design company with offices in Toronto and New York City, Farr also has a successful television career. She keeps transforming spaces and inspiring homeowners all around the world with her unique style and great taste..
