Orthodox Jewish model who shunned religious lifestyle set to star in new reality show

Just when it seemed that ideas for new reality shows had dried up along comes 'Shunned' - a 'docu-drama' following the lives of Orthodox Jews who have moved away from their strict religious faith.

The show features an aspiring model, her toy-boy lover and one Jewish man who is furious that his ultra-orthodox parents deprived him of bacon for the first 26 years of his life.

'When I had the first bite, I felt angry,' the 26-year-old Luzer Twersky said. 'I felt how could my parents keep this from me?'

Bringing home the bacon: Luzer Twersky, star of a new reality show, says his first bite of bacon made him angry that he had been deprived of it for so long

'Shunned': Twersky is starring in a new reality show about Jewish people leaving their strict religious lifestyles behind - he is now divorced and working in fashion

The show follows three Jewish New Yorkers who have ditched their Hasidic community.

Twersky's co-stars are striking 30-year-old divorcee Pearlperry Reich, who is hoping to launch her modelling career and her toy-boy lover.

Noah Sheinmann, an executive producer at No Regrets Entertainment, said the company is now shopping the show to the networks, and is still searching for a fourth cast member leaving the faith to sign up.

Similar to other reality shows 'Shunned' seems to focus on relationships and marital woes but Sheinmann argues that the appeal of the programme is that it shines a light on the Hasidic community, which, he argues, many people are ignorant about. 

Aspirations: Pearlperry Reich is taking part in the show with the hopes of launching her modelling and acting career

Past life: The show follows Reich as she divorces her husband and fights for custody of her children

All three people taking part in the show were in arranged marriages and are now either divorced or in the proceedings of getting divorced.

The star of the show looks set to be aspiring model and actress Pearlperry Reich, a glamorous mother-of-four who is in the middle of a custody battle over her children.

Model behaviour: Pearlperry Reich appears in the show with her toy-boy lover

The 30-year-old told the New York Post that she chose to be in the show ‘to create a positive Judaism' but quickly added that it would be ‘a really good opportunity for me to get my face out there.'

Photos show Reich’s sporting a mane of glossy blonde hair - a far cry from her look when she was still part of the Hasidic community.

Reich’s toy-boy lover, Shauli Grossman, 24, also stars in the show and said he joined to help educate Orthodox teens.

'There are a lot of people [for whom] this lifestyle is not a choice, and they would die to leave,' he said. 'We’re married off at 17 or 18 before we even know what we want.'

Twersky, who grew up in the Belz and Satmar sects in Borough Park, was married after his first date at 19 and is now divorced with two children.

He confessed to enjoying single life after separating from his wife but is now happily in a relationship with a blond, Irish-Catholic girl from Arkansas.

And as well as the revelatory taste of bacon Twersky has watched Star Wars for the first time since moving away from his strict Jewish faith.

'A lot of things are new to me. I saw ‘Star Wars’ for the first time last year,' he told the New York Post.

'It’s interesting. At least now I can understand what people are talking about; "May the force be with you." '
