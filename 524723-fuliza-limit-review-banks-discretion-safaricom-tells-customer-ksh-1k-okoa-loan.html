Safaricom has maintained that the Fuliza M-Pesa limit review is at the bank's discretion.

This followed a complaint raised by a customer claiming that his limit has remained zero, yet Okoa credit has increased to KSh 1,000.

How is Fuliza limit reviewed?

In response to TUKO.co.ke, the lender noted it cannot intervene to reset the limit, urging the customer to continue using M-Pesa services.

"The limits are approved at the bank's discretion, and we are not able to intervene and reset the limit. For the limit to increase, one must continue using Safaricom and M-PESA services frequently and save constantly without withdrawing. They should also keep their Safaricom line active and topped up once successful; the new limit will be communicated," said Safaricom.

In a similar inquiry on X, formerly Twitter, a customer shared a screenshot showing his Okoa limit of up to KSh 1,000.

"I believe this should be my Fuliza limit, which is trailing at zero," tweeted Mboya.

The telco noted that the Fuliza limit review lacks specific timelines.

"Based on your usage of airtime, M-Pesa and how you repay Okoa Jahazi is when the limit changes," the company explained.

Kenyans Fuliza credit worth KSh 19b

Safaricom customer concerns on zero Fuliza limits and increased Okoa came when the telco reported increased airtime purchased via the overdraft facility.

Safaricom PLC Sustainability Report 2023 showed Kenyans borrowed over 19 billion airtime from Fuliza.

Safaricom introduced the service in November 2022, allowing customers to borrow airtime through the overdraft facility.

The value of Fuliza disbursed for the year ending March 2023 grew by 39.6% to KSh 701.5 billion.

M-Pesa revenue increased by 8.8% to KSh 117.2 billion during the same period under review.
