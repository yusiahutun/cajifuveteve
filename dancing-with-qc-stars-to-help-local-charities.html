Dancing With QC Stars to help local charities

Publish date: 2024-03-07

The “Dancing With the Quad Cities Stars” event Thursday in Eldridge will help raise money for many area charities.

The dance competition will kick off at 7:30 p.m. Thursday, Sept. 22, at Eldridge Community Center, 400 S. 16th Ave., Eldridge, with a VIP Party at 6 p.m.

According to operations manager Kara Primmer, each participant is raising money for their charity of choice. There will be 12 local competitors and 12 charities present and represented. Thirty percent of each ticket sold goes to charity.

The show format is just like the TV show “Dancing With the Stars.” Each participant performs a routine previously performed on the show. Judging is 50% audience votes and 50% judge’s scores. The winner receives a mirror ball trophy. The event motto is “You don’t have to be a star to dance like one.”

The more people that a participant is able to invite equals more votes and more money raised for their charity. Each QC star is paired with an accomplished partner that they recruit from local talented dancers here in the Quad Cities.

The contestants, with their charity of choice, are:

  • Adena “Dee” Vilt (home care), Scott County Humane Society, “Pets for Vets.”
  • Hollywood Templeton (life coach), National Center for Missing and Exploited Children.
  • Jenny Colvin (chief development officer for River Bend Food Bank), River Bend.
  • John Huber (operations supervisor for Bethany for Children and Families), Bethany.
  • Katie Enlove (client services associate for Visionpoint Advisory Group), QC Autism Center.
  • Krystal Schaefer (real estate loan officer), Habitat for Humanity.
  • Lee Gonzales (social influencer), King’s Harvest Pet Rescue.
  • Marty Coleman (organic gardener), Scott County YMCA.
  • Pratina Khatri (actress), Davenport Junior Theatre.
  • Robyn Gehrke (substitute teacher and student at Western Governor’s University), Rescued.
  • Sue Erredge (podiatry specialist in Iowa City), Disabled American Veterans.
  • Suhaja Siri (paraeducator at Hopewell Elementary School), New Beginnings Pet Rescue.

Tickets for Thursday’s show only are $30, and $65 for VIP tickets, available HERE.

VIP tickets include:

  • Admission to the show at 7:30 p.m., and access to the pre-show VIP cocktail reception at 6 p.m., where you
    will be able to meet and greet the stars of the show and enjoy complimentary beer, wine, and 
    hors d’oeuvres.
  • Premiere seating alongside the stage at a VIP Table with eight people per table.
