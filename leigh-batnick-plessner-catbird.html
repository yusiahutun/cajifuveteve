Leigh Batnick Plessner of Catbird

Publish date: 2024-07-17

Media Platforms Design Team

How did you become a buyer?

I studied English and anthropology as an undergraduate and I ended up becoming a buyer at a bookstore before I started working at Catbird. That was eight years ago when there were no rules, and then I became the buyer and general manager. It was a slow progression of adding beauty into our assortment and I think as my relationship with beauty products changed and as my personal interest grew I began bringing more products in and exploring more for us. It felt really complementary to the Catbird world, which is about things that are meant to make you happy and feel good, things that are purely for joy and pleasure.

Media Platforms Design Team

What were some of the first brands you brought in?

Tocca was one of the first. It was the first luxurious candle Rony [the owner of Catbird] and I had bought for ourselves and it felt so special to us, so we brought in their candles and perfume. We also picked up D.S. & Durga right when they launched because I sold at the Brooklyn Flea next to their station. One of the real turning points was when we got Rodin. Rony and I had tried her stuff and loved it, so that was exciting.

What's your method for finding niche brands and choosing what to bring in?

I scour everywhere. Instagram is huge, I'll just go down a rabbit hole and find things to call in, but over time, we've been getting a lot more submissions from brands who want to be in the store. It's a big challenge to find something that's not mass market or widely available but still feels really complete from A to Z and not only is a beautiful product with a great name but with packaging that feels finished and special.
