CM Punk 'DID suffer a significant injury' during Royal Rumble match, as the VOCAL critic of Vince Mc

Publish date: 2024-09-01

CM Punk's hell-freezes-over return to WWE after a decade of exile where he publicly burned bridges with the sports-entertainment mecca had a singular goal with little resistance — headlining WrestleMania. 

Punk's main-event spot at WWE's biggest annual show is now in doubt due to reports of the 45-year-old suffering a significant injury during Saturday's 'Royal Rumble' event. 

Punk, a well-known critic of former WWE chairman Vince McMahon, competed in the show's namesake match against 29 other competitors, with one spot going badly, leading the referee to check on him. Punk replied 'my triceps,' per the Wrestling Observer Newsletter. 


Punk's torn right triceps was confirmed by PWInsider, with the 'Second City Saint' set to address the injury on tonight's episode of 'Monday Night RAW.' Surgery would put Punk out of action for several months. 

As the show came to a close, Punk can be seen sitting down, holding his arm and looking dejected. Besides any pain in his triceps, Punk lost his televised return to WWE after Cody Rhodes eliminated him from the men's Royal Rumble match last. 

Punk returned to WWE in November but suffered a torn right triceps injury on Saturday night

McMahon had a vocal critic in Punk well before he resigned from WWE's parent company TKO

WWE nor Punk himself have confirmed the injury. The injury looks to have occurred when taking a Future Shock DDT from Drew McIntyre, where Punk immediately rolled away from the action holding his arm after taking the move.

The winner of the Royal Rumble match receives a world-championship match at WrestleMania. Punk's goal of headlining the extravaganza was not gone due to WWE expanding the show to two nights in 2020. 

Now, the biggest hole in Punk's Hall of Fame resume will be delayed at least one more year, after the five-time WWE world champion never headlined WrestleMania during his first run with the company.  

Punk's bombastic nature that eventually made him quit WWE in January 2014 started with his 'pipe bomb' promo in June 2011. During the extremely rare, unscripted tangent, Punk degradingly called McMahon 'a millionaire who should be a billionaire.'

McMahon stepped down from WWE parent company TKO last Friday amid claims from former employee Janel Grant that she was sexually abused and trafficked by him to his wrestlers and executives. Also implicated in the new allegations is former WWE executive John Laurinaitis.

McMahon's first retirement from WWE happened in July 2022, after the Wall Street Journal revealed more than $12 million in hush payments McMahon had given out over the years, with Laurinaitis being let go from the company around that time for similar claims being made against him. 

The 78-year-old McMahon returned to the company last January with the intent of selling WWE, which he did to Endeavor for $9.2 billion in April 2023. 

McMahon has denied Grant's claims made against him, saying in a statement to DailyMail.com: 'I stand by my prior statement that Ms. Grant's lawsuit is replete with lies, obscene made-up instances that never occurred, and is a vindictive distortion of the truth.'

Punk's promo cuts from more than a decade became noteworthy, again, with the recent allegations.  

'You know why he’s not a billionaire? Because he surrounds himself with glad-handed, nonsensical, d*****bag [censored] yes men, like John Laurinaitis, who’s going to tell him everything he wants to hear,' Punk said.  

Punk returned to WWE last November after being fired for alleged backstage behavior with rival All Elite Wrestling. 

A torn triceps is Punk's third major injury since returning to professional wrestling in August 2021 with AEW. In May 2022, Punk suffered a foot injury that needed surgery. In September 2022, Punk tore his left triceps and was out nine months. 

Punk's participation at the 'Royal Rumble' was his third match back with the company, wrestling on two house shows in December against Dominik Mysterio.
