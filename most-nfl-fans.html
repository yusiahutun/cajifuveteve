9. Chicago Bears

  • Established Date: September 17, 1920
  • Head Coach: Matt Nagy
  • Titles Received: 9; NFL Championship (8) Super Bowl Championships (1) (as of 2021)
  • Best Players: Khalil Mack, Walter Payton, Dick Butkus, Richard Dent, and many more. 

Founded in Decatur, Illinois, on September 17, 1920, the Chicago Bears is one of the only two remaining members of the NFL’s founding (1920). As a league’s NFC (National Football Conference) member, the team competes in the NFL every season. 

The bears have a couple of significant achievements to their names. Achievements ranging from eight NFL Championship wins to winning a Super Bowl seem unstoppable as it is recorded for the most wins ever in the NFL.

Similarly, they also hold the record for most enshrinees in the Pro Football Hall of Fame, along with the most retired jerseys.

The Bears have been playing their home games at Soldier Field. Being on the 9th number of having the most NFL fans, the Chicago Bears have around 3.9 million fans.

Their fans are always ready to pack up Soldier Field any Sunday to cheer for their favorite team.

8. Seattle Seahawks

  • Established Date: June 4, 1974
  • Head Coach: Pete Carroll
  • Titles Received: 1 Super Bowl Championship (as of 2021)
  • Best Players: Russell Wilson, Walter Jones, Steve Largent, Bobby Wagner, and others. 

Based in Seattle, the Seattle Seahawks are members of the NFL’s NFC West club. The team has won 11 division titles and three conference championships. In addition, the Seahawks are the only ones to have competed in both NFC and AFC championship games. 

Notorious for having one of the biggest and the loudest fanbases in the U.S. Consisting of around 3.9 million fans, they are also called the “12th Man”, “12th Fan”, or “12s”.

The 12s set the Guinness Book of World Records twice for the loudest noise at a sports event. The first one is 136.6 decibels; in a match against the San Francisco 49ers in September 2013.

The second one is registering at 137.6 decibels; in a game against New Orleans Saints in December. 

Due to being the only NFL team based in the Pacific Northwest region, they get support from areas such as Alaska, Utah, Montana, Oregon, and Idaho. They also get permission from Canadian provinces like Saskatchewan, Alberta, and British Columbia.

7. New Orleans Saints

  • Established Date: November 1, 1966
  • Head Coach: Sean Payton 
  • Titles Received: 1 Super Bowl Championship (as of 2021)
  • Best Players: Trey Hendrickson, Drew Brees, Rickey Jackson, etc.

New Orleans Saints compete in the NFL every season as an NFC pro football league member. Although being the least successful team in their first few decades, with 20 seasons in a row with no victories, they do have quite a fan following.

After their success in 2009, when they won the Super Bowl, the team’s fans and followers reached the skies. 

Currently, the Saints come on the 7th list of the most NFL fans, with a fanbase of 4 million. The city of New Orleans’s community has always been a sport-supporting community.

The presence of the New Orleans Saints has always played a significant role in bringing the community together. Due to this, everyone in their city loves them soo much. 

6. San Francisco 49ers

Established in 1944, the “San Francisco 49ers” is a professional American Football team. The 49ers compete in the NFL as a member of the NFC West division. Similarly, they play their home games in Levi’s Stadium. 

Throughout the years, the team has set and won multiple titles ranging from; most games won in a season (18), most field goals in a season (44), most consecutive away games won (18), and most points scored in the Super Bowl(55), most touchdowns in a Super Bowl (8), most consecutive game scored (from 1979 to 2004), most field goals in a season (44), most points scored in a single postseason (126) and many other.

With achievements and titles such as the 49ers, there’s no doubt they have big numbers in the fans and followers department. Being ranked 6th in the most NFL fans list, the 49ers have a fanbase of around 4 million.

One of the best players of the 49ers, Joe Montana, made sure that many people jumped on the 49er’s ship since they have plenty of fans from all parts of the world.

5. Denver Broncos

  • Established Date: August 14, 1959
  • Head Coach: Vic Fangio
  • Titles Received: 3 Super Bowl Championships (as of 2021)
  • Best Players: Drew Lock, John Elway, Peyton Manning, Terrell Davis, etc. 

The Denver Broncos is a professional American football team with three championships. 

The Broncos didn’t do great in their 10-year run in the AFL and first seven years in the NFL. Their success started to lighten up only after the 1975 season. Since then, the team has won multiple titles to its name. The team has won 8 AFC championships and 3 Superbowls. 

Even though the Denver Broncos isn’t the brightest team in the league, their fan base has been loyal and supportive. With a fan following of 4.1 million, the Broncos come 5th on today’s list.

However, with such a loyal and devoted fanbase, the Broncos can surely count on their fans in times of need. 

4. Green Bay Packers

  • Established Date: August 11, 1919
  • Head Coach: Matt LaFleur
  • Titles Received: 15; NFL Championship (11) Super Bowl Championships (4) (as of 2021)
  • Best Players: Aaron Rodgers, Don Hutson, Bart Starr, Brett Favre, and more. 

Green Bay Packers is the third oldest American football team in the league. Being established in 1919, the team joined the NFL in 1921. The Packers compete in the NFL as a member club of the NFC North division. 

The Green Bay Packers have won a total of 15 championships so far. This number of wins is the most championships a team has ever succeeded in in NFL history. Before the AFL-NFL merger, the Packers were the only NFL team ever to defeat the AFL. 

The Packers have soo much history in the sport that the Vince Lombardi Trophy is named after Vince Lombardi, the Packers coach. 

With so much history and reputation in the sport, no wonder they get soo much love and attention. Their home base is the legendary Lambeau Field. The fans and followers count, reaching 5.2 million; they are the 4th on today’s list. 

3. Pittsburgh Steelers

Based in Pittsburgh, the Pittsburgh Steelers is a professional American football team in the NFL league. It is a member of the AFC North division. Established in 1933, the Steelers are among the most successful NFL franchises. 

Tied with the New England Patriots, it has the most Super Bowl titles (6). Similarly, the Steelers have played 16 times and hosted 11 times more conference championship games than any other team in the NFL, also winning eight AFC championships. 

The reputation of the Pittsburgh Steelers and the love and support they get has reached across the globe. Their fanbase is known as the Steeler Nation. The Steelers have fans from Mexico, Europe, to South America; they number around 6.3 million. 

The Steelers can always count on their Steeler Nation. Even though they are not in the game or out of the playoff, fans are always there to cheer for them and uphold them. That’s how strong the Steeler Nation is.

2. New England Patriots

  • Established Date: November 16, 1959
  • Head Coach: Bill Belichick
  • Titles Received: 6 Super Bowl Championships (as of 2021)
  • Best Players: Tom Brady, John Hannah, Rob Gronkowski, Andre Tippett, and others. 

Coming 2nd on the list, New England Patriots is one of the professional American football teams playing in the NFL league. The Boston-based team is a member of the AFC East division.

They have been playing their home games at Gillette Stadium. Similarly, they are one of the only two NFL teams representing two states. 

The New England Patriots also hold the record for most Super Bowl wins (6), losses (5), and appearances (11), followed by five championships to their name.

Some other records held by the Patriots are; the most playoff wins (37), most consecutive winning seasons (19 in total, from 2001 to 2019), the longest winning streak of the regular season, and playoff games (21, from 2003 to 2004). Along with these, the team has other titles to themselves.

With all these achievements, there isn’t a doubt that they come with a big fan following. The Patriots have a fan following of 7 million. The Patriots keep getting popular every season, every game. It won’t be long until the Patriots take the number one spot in the most NFL fans list.

1. Dallas Cowboys

  • Established Date: January 20, 1960
  • Head Coach: Mike McCarthy 
  • Title Received: 5 Super Bowl Championships (as of 2021)
  • Best Players: Zack Martin, Cliff Harris, Larry Allen, Darren Woodson, etc.

Standing at the number one spot in today’s list is the Dallas Cowboys. The proud team, backed by 8.5 million supporters, boasts their fanbase in every game.

As an NFC East division member, the Cowboys play their home games at the AT&T stadium. 

It was the year 2015 when the Dallas Cowboys became the most valuable sports team in the world, with the group valued at $4 billion. They also broke the record for a U.S. sports team’s annual revenue ($620 million).

Later in 2018, they broke their previous record of the most valuable sports team, valued at $5 billion. The Dallas Cowboys are also called America’s team.
