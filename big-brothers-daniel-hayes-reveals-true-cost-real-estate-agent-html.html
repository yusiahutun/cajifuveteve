Big Brother's Daniel Hayes reveals the true cost of being a real estate agent

Publish date: 2024-02-17

Daniel Hayes didn't hold back from flaunting his luxurious life during his appearance on Big Brother Australia last year.

And now the 48-year-old has spoken about how making deals to secure wealth meant he was never home with his wife and children.

Speaking to The Courier Mail on Sunday, Daniel said that his son Harry forced him to confront the choices he was making.

True to him: Big Brother's Daniel Hayes, 48, (pictured) has revealed the true cost of being a real estate agent: 'Ego has an unquenchable thirst' 

'My wife and I were fighting again and he said: 'Dad, isn't it enough?',' Hayes recalled.

'Why can't you just be home?' And what he meant was; look at everything we have, private schools, we had everything materially, but they never had their dad.'

Hayes explained that his success cost him two marriages. 

Life story: Speaking to The Courier Mail on Sunday, Daniel said that his son Harry forced him to confront the choices he was making

'Some people just make you think that real estate is life, it's a drug, doing a deal selling at an auction under the hammer and the crowd cheer you and the vendor is crying and you are king for a day, you're a God. 

'Unfortunately ego has an unquenchable thirst. It'll take everything from you. Everything,' he added.

Meanwhile, previously speaking to Daily Mail Australia, Daniel admitted despite being shown in a 'bad light' on Big Brother, you won't hear see him whingeing.

No editing tricks: The Geelong-based real estate agent told Daily Mail Australia: 'I think that's who I am. They've shown me in a pretty bad light, but I accept the camera's don't lie'

'I think that's who I am. They've shown me in a pretty bad light, but I accept the camera's don't lie,' he said.

'There's good and bad about me,' he added. 'I'm a human being and you get to see the guy who shoots from the hip.' 

Describing his time in the Big Brother house, Daniel continued: 'I try to be funny. I try to lift the house up and I was prepared to go home at any day.

'I was there to play a big game.' 

In it to win it: 'I wasn't there to make friends. I've got enough of those and they're high-maintenance as it is,' Daniel said
